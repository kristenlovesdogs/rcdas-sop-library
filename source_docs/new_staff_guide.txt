Welcome to RCDAS
Welcome to the Riverside County Department of Animal Services. As a staff member, you serve as the primary point of contact between the public and our animal services. This manual provides essential knowledge and procedures needed to assist the public and coordinate services across all programs effectively.
About RCDAS
RCDAS is one of the largest shelter systems in the nation, taking in between 30,000 and 40,000 animals each year. RCDAS operates four campuses. The Jurupa Valley campus is the largest and houses the majority of the administrative staff. The San Jacinto facility is the second-largest, followed by the Coachella Valley (CVAC) campus. The Blythe campus is located near the Arizona border and primarily serves as an intake facility for this remote part of the County. 
What We Do


* Stray and lost pets. We take in stray and lost pets and help them get back to their owners. 
* Owned pets. We also help struggling pet owners keep their pets and sometimes take in pets that their owners must surrender. 
* Adoptions. We adopt thousands of pets to loving homes each year. 
* Volunteer. We work with volunteers to help provide the best possible care to animals staying at our shelters.
* Foster. We work with foster caregivers to expand our shelter capacity while they provide medical care and time, and assist with placement by providing pet information and marketing.
* Rescue. We work with rescue and shelter partners to save the lives of the most vulnerable animals.
* Euthanasia. We provide end-of-life euthanasia services to people who cannot afford to take their pet to a shelter or private veterinarian.
* Sick and injured animals. Our vet team cares for sick and injured animals, as well as orphaned puppies and kittens. 
* Animal care. We provide clean housing, food, and medicine for 1,000 or more animals each day. 
* Animal control and field services. Our animal control officers investigate cases of cruelty, neglect, and hoarding. They also work to protect public safety by removing dangerous animals from the community. Officers also respond to routine nuisance and stray animal complaints. 
* Spay and neuter. We offer spay and neuter services for owned pets in under-resourced parts of our community and provide microchips and tags to these animals. 
* Trap-Neuter-Return.  We provide free sterilization, ear tipping, and vaccination for outdoor cats to prevent unwanted litters. 
Roles and Responsibilities 


There are more than 250 employees across four campuses. Most people fall into one of the following position categories: 


* Animal care technician (ACT)
* Animal service representative (ASR)
* Animal service counselor (ASC)
* Veterinary staff, including registered veterinary technicians (RVT) and veterinary assistants (VA)
* Animal control officer (ACO)
* Dispatchers
* Administrative and executive staff (various positions)
* Temporary Assignment Program (TAP)


Expectations 


1. Treat every person and animal with respect and kindness. 
2. If you don’t know something, ask instead of guessing. 
3. Know your limits to keep everyone safe. Don’t do something that would put your safety or the safety of you or others at risk. 
4. If you have a problem you can’t resolve, talk to your supervisor or the on-duty supervisor. 
5. Make customer service a priority and continuously strive to improve the customer experience, both internally and externally.
6. Set others up for success by sharing information and helping each other.
7. Treat every animal as if its life and well-being are as important as your own pets at home.

	Definitions


Activity: A service request or case created in the Chameleon system for field officers or staff to complete (such as responding to a stray animal complaint or conducting a welfare check).


Animal Control Officer (ACO): Field staff who respond to animal-related calls, investigate complaints, and enforce animal laws.


Animal ID: Unique identification number assigned to each animal that enters the RCDAS system.


CalTrans: California Department of Transportation, responsible for deceased animal removal on state highways and freeways.


Chameleon: RCDAS's shelter software database that manages shelter operations by tracking animals, owners, activities, and case management.


Cisco Finesse: Phone system software used for call center operations.


Community cat (aka stray cat or free-roaming cat)t: Cats are not required to be licensed or registered in the State of California. Cats that live outdoors, either part of the time or all the time, are considered community cats. 


Community Foster: Temporary care for stray animals by volunteers in their own homes instead of bringing them to the shelter, which increases the chances of reuniting with owners.


Contracted Cities: Incorporated cities that contract with Riverside County Department of Animal Services (RCDAS) to provide animal control field services, animal sheltering services, or both.


Cross Streets: The intersecting streets nearest to a specific address, used to help locate exact positions.


CVAC: Coachella Valley Animal Campus, one of four RCDAS shelter facilities.


Diversion: Process of offering alternatives to pet surrender, including resources and support services to help owners keep their animals.


End-of-Life Services: Humane euthanasia services provided to pet owners who cannot afford private veterinary care.


Euthanasia: The humane ending of an animal's life, typically due to severe illness, injury, or behavioral issues.


Extension Request: A request to extend the deadline for paying an animal control citation.


Feral: Wild or untamed animals that avoid human contact, typically referring to cats that were not socialized to humans.


GEO Codes: Geographic reference codes used to identify specific locations within the county.


HPZ: Healthy Pet Zone program providing veterinary care, supplies, and support to help families keep their pets during difficult times.


Intake: The process of receiving and documenting animals brought into the shelter system.


Jurisdiction: Geographic area served by RCDAS (unincorporated Riverside County plus contracted cities).


Kennel: Individual housing unit for animals in the shelter; also refers to the department that manages daily animal care.


Kennel License: License issued by DAS that allows more than four dogs to be maintained on a property. 


Microchip: A Small electronic device implanted under an animal's skin that contains identification information.


Neonate: Newborn animals, typically referring to puppies or kittens under four weeks of age.


Noisy Animal Declaration: A formal complaint document required to initiate noise violation proceedings against pet owners.


Official Notice (Green Card): Door hanger used by ACOs after visiting a property and not making contact with a person. 


OOJ: Out of Jurisdiction - areas not served by RCDAS, requiring referral to other agencies.


Owner Surrender: When pet owners voluntarily give up their animals to the shelter.


Person ID (PID): ID number used to identify individuals in the Chameleon database system.


Priority Levels: Classification system for field activities based on urgency (1=Emergency, 2=Urgent, 3=Assist Fire/Law, 4=Must be responded to same day, 5=Routine).


Rabies Control: A program that monitors and prevents rabies transmission, including bite investigations and quarantine procedures.


RCDAS: Riverside County Department of Animal Services.


RVT: Registered Veterinary Technician, licensed animal healthcare professional.


SNR: Shelter-Neuter-Return program for friendly stray cats that are spayed/neutered, then adopted or returned to their territory.


Spay/Neuter: Surgical procedures to prevent animal reproduction (spay for females, neuter for males).


Stray: Lost or abandoned animals found roaming without owners.


Surrender Fee: A fee charged to owners who surrender their pets to the shelter, intended to cover care costs.


Timestamp: Date and time notation added to database records to track when actions occurred.


TNR: Trap-Neuter-Return program for feral community cats that are captured, sterilized, and returned to their territory to control the population.


Trap: A humane cage device used to capture cats for TNR programs safely.


Unincorporated County: Areas of Riverside County not within city limits, served directly by county government rather than municipal services.


Violation Number: Unique identifier assigned to each citation or violation case.


Welfare Check: Investigation to assess whether animals are receiving proper care, food, water, and shelter.

Section 2: Call Handling Procedures
Information Collection Standards


For every call, collect the following minimum information:


* Exact address and cross streets
* Age of caller (confirm age of caller - must be an adult 18+)
* Caller name and address (get phone, and email when possible)
* Detailed description of situation - Who, what, where, when, why
* Animal description (size, breed, color, behavior)
* Relevant Animal ID numbers
* Timeframe of observed issues
Priority System for Field Activities


Priority 1 Emergency: Threat to human life or safety


* An aggressive animal is currently attacking a human
* Rattlesnakes (must maintain visual)
* Deceased or alive bats in homes
* Must have visual confirmation
* Immediate public safety threat


Priority 2 Urgent: Threat to animal life or safety


* Extreme cruelty or neglect - the animal may die without intervention
* Injured or sick animals requiring immediate attention
* Aggressive animals are preventing normal activities
* Animal blocking traffic


Priority 3: same-day response


* Animals tied up
* Law enforcement assist (including fire department, CHP, public assistance)
* Traffic hazards
* Lose livestock on streets or train tracks
* Animals in distress


Priority 4: same-day response


* The animal is confined, requiring increased attention


Priority 5 (Routine Response):


* Stray roam
* Stray habit
* Deceased animal
* Inv kennel
* Inv bite (not within the last hour, and the animal is not loose or stray). 
* Disposal requests
* General welfare checks
Emergency Situations
For active emergencies involving aggressive animals, immediate public safety threats, or emergency assist requests from law enforcement/fire department, transfer calls directly to Dispatch at extension x87350

Section 3: Specific Call Types and Procedures
Lost and Found Animals


Lost Pet Procedure:


1. Ask if the animal is microchipped
2. If microchipped, then search system immediately
3. Direct the caller to the RCDAS lost and found page to file a report with Petco Love Lost
4. Inform the caller that they can walk through the kennels to search for their lost pet
5. Inform the caller of website updates every 30 minutes
6. Direct the caller to follow the steps outlined on the RCDAS website
7. Provide email for support: lostandfoundDAS@rivco.org


Found Pet Procedure:


1. Determine whether the animal is visibly sick or injured, or poses a threat to public or finder safety.
2. Ask the caller if they can hold the animal they found.
3. If the caller cannot keep the animal, provide them instructions for bringing the animal to the shelter
   1. If the caller is unable to drive or otherwise unable to get the animal to the shelter, create a pickup activity
4. Recommend microchip scanning at a veterinarian, PetSmart, Petco, or shelter
5. Direct to petcolovelost.org registration and social media posting
Community Cat Program


Services Available:


* Trap-Neuter-Return (TNR) for community cats (services vary based on location. See current TNR flyer) 
* Working Cat Program to find barn homes for cats that are feral but cannot be released to where they came from. 
* Community Foster support


TNR Program Guidelines (as of December 19, 2024):


* Walk-in TNRs limited to 10 cats per day, Monday-Friday
* First-come, first-served basis
* 2 cats per person maximum
* Must live in the RCDAS jurisdiction with a valid ID
* Check-in times: 8:00-8:30 AM, must be present with the cat
* Required: Cats must arrive in proper cat traps only
* San Jacinto - by appointment only. 
* Direct people to the RCDAS website.


Community Cat Inquiries:


1. Assess situation: feral colony, nuisance complaints, or population control needs
2. For feral cat complaints: Offer TNR services and community education
3. For stray cats: Consider the SNR program or community foster
4. Schedule site assessment if needed
Animal Complaints


Noisy Animal Complaints:
* Instruct the complainant to visit https://rcdas.org/noisy-animals 
* Require a formal Declaration of Noisy Animal form
* Available at RCDAS.org under Services → Forms
* NOT an anonymous process - information will be shared
* The owner receives a warning notice first
* If the complainant does not have access to the internet, staff can mail out a complaint letter, and a notice can be mailed back or emailed. 
* Questions: DASnoisyanimals@rivco.org


Stray Animals: Create an activity with detailed information:
* Exact location with landmarks
* Animal description and behavior
* The duration the animal has been present
* Whether the animal appears injured, aggressive, or contained


Animal Welfare Concerns: Document specific observations:
* Lack of food, water, or shelter
* Visible injuries or illness
* Timeframes for observed conditions
* The caller must have an address or cross streets
* Must be reported by an observer; no third-party calls are permitted.
* Number of animals (over 4 dogs or 9 cats requires permits)
   * An INV KENNEL can be created for complaints on number of animals


Deceased Animal (not owned by caller):
* Exact location, including cross streets, address, and where exactly the animal is. Example- in the road, next to the big tree, under the patio table. 
* Type of animal
* Freeways: Refer to CalTrans (909-383-4631)
* Under Homes: RCDAS does not provide this service


Livestock:
* Refer to the County code

Dibs program (pre-adoption)


Animals Eligible:  Any dog that is marked “stray wait” or “known wait”.  Cats are not eligible, as there is no stray holding period for them.


Script for animals eligible for DIBS


Thank you for your interest in (Animal Name) (A#).  This pet is currently on stray hold, awaiting their family to find them; however, if you would like to adopt them, you may place DIBS on this pet by visiting in person (Shelter Name) (Shelter Address) anytime during business hours, which are Tuesday - Friday 11 am - 6 pm, and Saturday and Sunday 10 am - 4 pm. 


All pets are available on a first-come, first-served basis for the DIBS program and adoption.  RCDAS can not guarantee the availability of any pet.
Pets on DIBS will remain at the shelter until the stray hold has expired. If an owner is identified or reunited with their pet at any time during the stray hold, the pet is no longer eligible for DIBS.  Pets with DIBS must be picked up by the end of the business day once they become the property of RCDAS.


Thank you, and we look forward to seeing you soon.


If an animal is marked “available”, let the caller know that the pet can be adopted immediately.

Thank you for your interest in (Animal Name) (A#).  This pet is currently available for adoption. You can learn more or adopt this pet by visiting the in person (Shelter Name) (Shelter Address) anytime during business hours, which are  Tuesday - Friday 11 am - 6 pm, and Saturday and Sunday 10 am - 4 pm. 
All pets are available for adoption on a first-come, first-served basis.  RCDAS can not guarantee the availability of any pet.

Section 5: Standard Scripts


General Guidelines


1. Answer the phone with this greeting: "Department of Animal Services, this is NAME; how can I help you?"  
2. Start with thank you. This is true regardless of why someone is reaching out. “Thank you so much for reaching out to us.” 
3. Let them know you’re there to help. “This is also important regardless of why they’re reaching out. “Let’s see how we can best help you today.” 
4. Be honest and open. “I don’t have a simple solution for that problem, but I’m going to offer you a couple of options.” 
5. Offer affirmation. “I hear you.” “That makes a lot of sense.” 
6. Set clear boundaries and explain them. “We cannot house your animal at the shelter for you because we don’t offer boarding here. Our kennels are used for pets that are lost, stray, or have been surrendered by their owners.” 
7. Negotiate. “I understand you cannot keep your cat long term. Would you agree to post your cat on Rehome and reach out to family and friends to try to find a place for her? If you try for a week and still cannot find a place for her, we can make you a surrender appointment.”

Shelter Status Script:
“Our shelter is currently at capacity, meaning we have no available space for incoming pets. If this pet enters the shelter, it may face a risk of euthanasia due to a lack of kennel space. We aim to avoid euthanizing any healthy, adoptable pet and are seeking your assistance to save a life."


Found Pets Script
"Thank you for helping and caring for this lost pet. We want to help reunite this pet with its owner as quickly as possible. We're seeking the community's help as we are at capacity and have nowhere for incoming pets to go. Would you consider holding onto this pet to help it reunite with its owner? We can scan the pet for a microchip and help you get it registered so the owner can easily locate their pet online. If the owner isn't found, we can assist with spaying/neutering and vaccinations if you're open to rehoming the pet outside the shelter. Even a few days of care can be lifesaving during this critical time."


Found Pets Script (for the finder who may want to keep the found pet)
“Thank you so much for your willingness to help with this pet. All you have to do is file a found report with Petco Love Lost. This platform will notify a potential owner that you have found their pet and connect with them through a secure, private platform. We recommend that you also post this pet on local lost-and-found Facebook page(s), Nextdoor, and Pawboost to help the owner find their pet. After 30 days, you can get this pet licensed and microchipped in your name. You can find information about this on our website.

“I’m sorry to hear that your pet has gone missing. To report your pet as lost, fill out the “I Lost a Pet” form on Petco Love Lost (petcolovelost.org). We also recommend posting a lost pet on local lost-and-found Facebook page(s), Nextdoor, and Pawboost to increase awareness in the community.
