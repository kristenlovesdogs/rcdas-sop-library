Executive Summary
Executive Summary


Purpose of This Glossary
This data glossary defines all intake and outcome types used in the Riverside County Department of Animal Services (RCDAS) data management system (Chameleon).  Accurate coding and data collection are essential for transparent reporting, regulatory compliance, and improving outcomes for animals in our care.


Why Accurate Data Matters
* Reporting and Transparency:  Accurate data enables meaningful public reporting and demonstrates our commitment to transparency in animal welfare
* Resource Allocation:  Proper classification helps identify trends and allocate resources effectively
* Legal Compliance:  Many intake and outcome types have specific legal requirements under California State Law
* Live Release Rate:  Correct coding ensures accurate calculation of our live release rate, a key performance metric

v1. Glossary - Intake and Outcome Types
Intake Types
Intake types describe how animals enter the RCDAS shelter system (Chameleon).  Each intake type has specific subtypes that provide additional detail about the circumstances of the intake.  You will find intake and outcome types within Chameleon’s kennel window.
CONFISCATE
Definition:  Animals seized or confiscated by authorities (e.g., peace officers, humane society officers, animal control officers, or law enforcement) for various legal reasons and housed at RCDAS for a period mandated by state or local law.  Animals may be owned or stray.  See Penal Code section 597.1 under California State Law.


When to Use: Use CONFISCATE when legal authority removes an animal from a situation, rather than the owner voluntarily surrendering or a stray being picked up routinely.
        
Subtype
	Definition
	Example
	ABANDONMNT
	Animal intentionally left by its owner without providing care or support  
	Dog chained in vacant lot with no food or water; owner identified but refused to reclaim
	AN CONTROL
	Animal confiscated by an animal control officer, from a contract city (sheltering only)
	Contract city officer seizes animal for hoarding case
	BITE
	Animal confiscated due to bite incident involving another person or another animal
	Dog bites neighbor; animal is confiscated for quarantine and investigation
	CRUELTY
	Animal confiscated due to cruelty or neglect 
	Severely emaciated horses seized by authorities
	EVICTION
	Animal confiscated when owner is evicted from property
	Cats left behind after landlord eviction; sheriff requests animal control assistance
	HOSPITAL
	Animal seized when owner is hospitalized and unable to arrange care
	Owner has medical emergency; no family available to care for pets
	OWNER DIED
	Animal confiscated due to death of owner
	Deceased person’s animal discovered by authorities; no next of kin available
	POLICE
	Police confiscated animal and brought to shelter
	Police responding to domestic violence call find injured animals at scene
	PUB SAFETY
	Animal confiscated due to threat of public safety
	Aggressive dog repeatedly escaping yard and chasing children at bus stop
	SPEC CIRC
	Special circumstance such as disaster response (fire,flooding, earthquake)
	Wildfire evacuation; animals rescued from danger zone
	OWNER SUR (Owner Surrender)
Definition:  Animal voluntarily brought to the shelter and relinquished by its owner, including adoption returns.


When to Use: Use OWNER SUR when the owner is making a conscious decision to give up the animal.


Subtype
	Definition
	Example
	> 30 DAYS
	Owner surrendered animal more than 30 days after adoption
	Cat adopted 2 months ago returned due to behavior issues
	AN CONTROL
	Owner surrendered to animal control officer from contract city
	Contract city officer accepts surrender from owner and delivers animal to RCDAS shelter
	BITE
	Owner voluntarily surrendered animal after it bit another person or another animal
	Dog bites postal worker; owner brings dog to shelter voluntarily to surrender
	CRUELTY
	Owner voluntarily surrenders animal after or just prior to an Officer seizing the animal for a cited for animal cruelty violation
	Dog chained outside without shelter, food, or water; owner agrees to surrender animal to RCDAS
	FIELD
	Owner surrendered animal directly to an officer in the field rather than at the shelter
	Field Services Officer responds to a call about a repeatedly loose dog.  Upon arrival, officer speaks with owner, who admits they can no longer care for the dog and surrenders the animal to the field officer
	OTC
	Owner surrendered animal at shelter counter (Over The Counter)
	Family moving to an apartment that doesn’t allow pets
	ABANDONMENT
	Custodian voluntarily turned in animal by citing abandonment as the reason
	Landlord requests RCDAS take custody of animal left behind by tenant 
	



RETURN
Definition:  Animal adopted from RCDAS is returned to the shelter. 


When to Use: Use RETURN when the owner is returning an animal adopted from an RCDAS shelter.


Subtype
	Definition
	Example
	BITE
	Owner returns adopted animal due to bite incident
	Family adopts puppy and it bites child; family brings the dog back to shelter
	IN 30 DAYS
	Adopted animal returned within 30 days
	Dog adopted on December 15th, returned on December 17th
	

STRAY
Definition:  Animal stated to be unowned, free-roaming, or found without owner present.  Animal is held for legally mandated holding period to allow for owner reclaim.


When to Use: Use STRAY when an animal is found running at large or the ownership status is unknown at time of intake.


Subtype
	Definition
	Example
	ABANDONMNT
	Stray animal running a large, brought in by member of public
	Member of the public finds animal wandering in neighborhood with no owner in sight and brings it to shelter
	AN CONTROL
	Stray picked up by animal control officer from contract city
	Contract city animal control officer finds animal wandering in the street, with no owner in sight, no microchip, no collar/tag
	BITE
	Stray animal brought to shelter by member of the public due to bite incident
	A stray cat scratched and bit someone, and the finder brought it to the shelter for quarantine. 
	FIELD
	Stray animal picked up by RCDAS Field Services
	Field officer responds to report of loose dog in neighborhood
	OTC
	Stray animal brought to shelter by member of the public (Over The Counter)
	Good Samaritan finds injured cat and brings it to shelter
	DEAD
	Dead stray animal brought to shelter (not counted in live release rate)
	Citizen found a deceased dog on their property and brings it to shelter
	

TRANSFER (Transfer In)

Subtype

	Example

	DAS
	Animal transferred internally between RCDAS shelters (Blythe, CVAC, Jurupa Valley, San Jacinto)
	Blythe, primarily an intake shelter, transfers animals to CVAC to increase visibility and opportunities for live outcomes

	Outcome Types
Outcome types describe how animals exit the RCDAS shelter system.  Proper outcome coding is critical for accurate live release rate reporting and demonstrates our commitment to positive outcomes.  
ADOPTION
Definition: Animal custody permanently transferred from RCDAS to an individual adopter through the adoption process.


When to Use: Use when an approved adopter completes the adoption process and takes permanent custody of the animal.


Subtype
	Definition
	Example
	CONTRACT
	Animal adopted unaltered where a s/n deposit has been paid.
	Family adopts a dog or cat that is unaltered and pays a spay/neuter deposit to return for surgery
	NEW
	General adoption (most common adoption type)
	Family adopts dog from shelter
	OFF SITE
	Animal adopted at offsite location (not Petco, Petsmart, or adoption event)
	Animal displayed at community center and adopted
	PETCO
	RCDAS animal housed at Petco location was adopted
	Animal displayed at Petco adoption center adopted by customer
	PETSMART
	RCDAS animal housed at Petsmart location was adopted
	Animal displayed at Petsmart adoption center adopted by customer
	SURGWAIT
	Animal was adopted and is in shelter awaiting surgery before going home
	Cat adopted on Friday; scheduled for Monday surgery; will go home Tuesday
	

ADOPT EVNT (Adoption Event)
Definition: Animal custody permanently transferred from RCDAS to an individual adopter at an adoption event.


When to Use:  Use when tracking adoptions for a specific event or grant reimbursement.


Subtype
	Definition
	Example
	CONTRACT
	Animal adopted unaltered where a s/n deposit has been paid
	Family adopts a dog or cat that is unaltered and pays a spay/neuter deposit to return for surgery
	NEW
	General adoption (most common adoption type)
	Family adopts dog at RCDAS mega adoption event
	SURGWAIT
	Animal was adopted and is in shelter awaiting surgery before going home
	Family adopts a dog or cat and the animal stays in the shelter until surgery
	

RTO (Return to Owner)
Definition: Animal returned to its owner after being processed as an intake.


When to Use: Use when the original owner reclaims their animal from the shelter.


Subtype
	Definition
	Example
	ABANDONMENT
	Used for billing purposes when known owner is charged for time animal remained in custody after failing to  reclaim (NOT counted in live release rate)
	Owner identified but fails to reclaim within holding period; billing code only
	CHNGED MND
	The owner changed their mind about reclaiming the animal after starting the return-to-owner process
	Owner comes to reclaim their dog but, after learning of medical costs, decides not to take the animal home
	CONTRACT
	Animal RTO’d unaltered where a s/n deposit has been paid
	Family retrieves impounded, unaltered dog and pays spay/neuter deposit to return for surgery
	COUNTER
	Animal returned to owner at RCDAS shelter
	Owner comes to shelter, provides identification, and reclaims their lost dog
	FIELD
	Animal returned to owner in the field by Field Services officer
	Field officer picks up stray, verifies microchip, and returns animal directly to owner’s home
	SURGWAIT
	Animal RTO’d and is in shelter waiting on surgery
	Family retrieves impounded, unaltered dog and animal stays in the shelter until surgery

Definition: Animal custody transitioned to another organization or another RCDAS shelter.


Subtype
	Definition
	Example
	AN CONTROL
	Animal Transferred to another Animal Control Agency
	Dog is impounded by RCDAS and is transferred to an allied agency.
	ANML SAMAR
	Animal transferred to Animal Samaritans (animalsamaritans.org)
	Animals transferred to Animal Samaritan's rescue partner
	AT VET

	CONTRACT

	DAS

	Blythe transfers animals to CVAC to increase exposure and adoption opportunities for those animals
	LEAF

	OFF SITE

	SPCA
	Animal transferred to Mary S. Roberts (MSR) rescue
	Animal transferred to MSR
	

EUTH (Euthanasia)
Definition: Animal euthanized while in RCDAS custody.


When to Use: Use when an animal is humanely euthanized for medical, behavioral, or legal reasons.        


Subtype
	Definition
	Example
	AT VET
	Animal euthanized while at veterinary office
	ACO working Standby takes an injured animal to an after-hours vet and animal is euthanized.
	COURT ORD
	Animal euthanized under directive from a court or legal authority typically following ruling related to public safety, dangerous dog designation, or other legal proceedings
	Animal involved in a severe bite case is euthanized after judge issues a court order
	OWNER REQ
	Owner requests humane euthanasia due to medical or behavioral issue
	Shelter provides humane euthanasia for an owner’s senior pet suffering from chronic medical issues and poor quality of life
	RABIES TST
	Animal euthanized due to suspected rabies infection and submitted for FRA to confirm the presence of the rabies virus.
	A stray raccoon showing neurological symptoms is euthanized and sent for rabies testing.
	TOO YOUNG
	Animal humanely euthanized because it is too young to survive or thrive on its own and lacks a mother or suitable care option.
	A litter of newborn kittens without a mother and without a foster opportunity were euthanized because they were too young to survive on their own 
	TRT-BEHV
	Animal euthanized due to behavior issues that could have been improved or resolved in a typical home but were not manageable within the shelter setting.
	A long stay dog develops behavior issues while in shelter; foster and adoption are not available.
	TRT-MEDI
	Animal euthanized due to medical conditions that are treatable but cost-prohibitive or require resources beyond what the shelter can provide
	A cat impounded with highly contagious URI at an RCDAS shelter that the shelter veterinarian has determined can be treated with reasonable measures but requires resources beyond what the shelter can provide
	UNTRT-BEHV
	Animal euthanized due to severe behavior issues that are considered unsafe or untreatable, even with intervention or typical home
	A dog declared to be dangerous or vicious as defined by county ordinance or California state law during any legal hearing process
	UNTRT-MEDI
	Animal euthanized due to serious or terminal medical conditions that are not treatable and would significantly impact the animal’s quality of life
	Injured animal impounded at an RCDAS shelter that the shelter veterinarian deemed to be suffering and untreatable
	



DIED (Died In Care)
Definition: Animal died unassisted while in RCDAS custody.                                                
Subtype
	Definition
	Example
	AT VET
	Animal died naturally or was found deceased while under veterinary care, either during treatment or recovery at an offsite vet
	Standby ACO takes an injured animal to an after-hours vet and the injured animal dies while at the emergency clinic.
	ENROUTE
	Animal died while on its way to or from the shelter, veterinary clinic, or another facility 
	A dog that was hit by a car died on its way to the shelter before receiving veterinary care.
	IN FOSTER
	Animal died while placed in a foster home, either from natural causes, illness, or other circumstances
	A neonate kitten died in foster care despite supportive feeding and warming efforts.
	IN KENNEL
	Animal was found deceased in its kennel while in shelter’s care
	A cat was found deceased in its kennel during morning rounds, having died overnight from unknown causes.
	IN SURGERY
	Animal died during surgical procedure
	A cat undergoing a spay surgery experienced complications and died before the procedure was completed.
	RABIES TST
	Animal died and was submitted for rabies testing to determine if virus was present
	A raccoon that bit a dog was found dead and submitted for rabies testing.
	

Other Outcome Types
                                                
Type/Subtype
	Definition
	Example
	TNR[a]
	A community-based practice, distinct from shelter-based RTF, primarily for managing feral cat populations. Cats are humanely trapped, sterilized, vaccinated, ear-tipped, and returned to their original outdoor location, often under the care of a community caregiver. These cats are NOT counted as an intake or an outcome.
	A colony caretaker traps a cat, takes it to an RCDAS clinic for spay/neuter, vaccinations, and eartipping.  The colony caretaker then returns the cat to its found location.
	CCP[b]
	Community Cat means any free-roaming cat that may be cared for by one or more residents of the immediate area, whether known or unknown.  A community cat may or may not be feral.  These cats are counted as intakes and outcomes for the shelter
	Any healthy cat that was impounded into an RCDAS shelter that is spay/neutered, eartipped, and released.
	RTF/SNR[c]
	A shelter-based program for managing community cats. Healthy cats with no signs of ownership are brought to the shelter, sterilized, vaccinated, ear-tipped, and returned to the location where they were found.  These cats are counted as intakes and outcomes for the shelter
	Any healthy cat that was impounded into an RCDAS shelter deemed eligible to be spay/neutered, eartipped, vaccinated, and returned to its found location.  
	MISSING
	Animal outcome is unknown
	N/A

	A dog slipped out of its collar and ran off while being walked by a volunteer; despite search efforts, the dog was not located
	    IN FOSTER

	A foster reported that a cat escaped from the home though an open window and could not be found
	    IN KENNEL
	Animal unaccounted for within the shelter; records show is should be present but it cannot be located and there is no evidence of escape 
	During morning rounds, a cat listed as housed in Kennel X could not be found, and there were no signs of escape
	    STOLEN
	Animal stolen from RCDAS custody
	A dog was removed from its kennel overnight without authorization; security cameras recorded individual removing the animal 
	RELEASED[d]

	N/A
	    CCP CAT[e]
	Community Cat means any free-roaming cat that may be cared for by one or more residents of the immediate area, whether known or unknown.  A community cat may or may not be feral.
	Any healthy cat that was impounded into an RCDAS shelter that is spay/neutered, eartipped, and released.
	    EARTIPPED[f]
	Community Cat that is eartipped
	A field officer impounds (STRAY-FIELD) and releases a confined cat that is eartipped, indicating that the cat is a community cat that has been spay/neutered and vaccinated.
